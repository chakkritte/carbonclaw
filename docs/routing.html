Smart Task Routing

CarbonClaw's Smart Router dynamically selects the most appropriate AI model based on task type and complexity.

How it Works

When you provide a prompt, CarbonClaw performs three steps to decide where to route the request:

  1. Task Classification: Keywords in your prompt are matched against categories (Coding, Research, Slides, General).
  2. Complexity Estimation: Prompt length and structural indicators are analyzed to assign a complexity score (0.0 to 1.0).
  3. Provider Selection: Based on your strategy (Sustainability, Latency, or Balanced), the router picks a local or cloud provider.

Routing Strategies

You can switch strategies using the /strategy slash command:

  • /strategy sustainability (Default): Prioritizes local models (Ollama) for simple to medium tasks to minimize carbon footprint.
  • /strategy latency: Selects the fastest responding provider based on real-time performance metrics.
  • /strategy balanced: A mix of cost, speed, and quality factors.

Task-Specific Models

You can pin specific models to task types in your pyproject.toml:

[tool.carbonclaw.routing]
coding = "qwen2.5-coder:32b"
research = "qwen2.5:32b"
slides = "qwen2.5-coder:32b"
general = "llama3.2:3b"
fallback = "llama3.1:8b"
Adaptive Learning

CarbonClaw learns from every turn. It tracks average latency and error rates using Exponential Moving Average (EMA) to avoid unhealthy providers automatically.
